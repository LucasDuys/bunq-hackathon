## One-paragraph view

**Reserving money** for a future credit purchase is an internal accounting act -- no public claim can be made yet. **Purchasing** a credit at month end is the commercial transaction. **Retiring** the credit is when the tonnes can be claimed against emissions. Carbon Reserve is honest about this: the UI distinguishes "reserved", "purchased", "retired" as three stages with three different badges. The CSRD E1-7 export only counts retired tonnes in the "offsets" column; reserved + purchased-but-not-retired tonnes go into a separate "pipeline" column.

## Pitch language (honest and defensible)

> "Carbon Reserve does not claim emissions are offset the moment a transaction fires. It moves money into a ring-fenced Reserve, then at month end buys verified EU removal credits. Only retired tonnes are counted in the CSRD E1-7 export. Everything else is surfaced as pipeline."
