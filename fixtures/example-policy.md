# Acme BV carbon policy — draft v2

_Last reviewed: 2025-11-20 by the sustainability working group (B. Jansen, M. de Vries)._

## 1. Context

Acme BV is a Netherlands-registered software company with roughly 45 employees and a single Amsterdam office. This policy covers the **bank-visible portion of our emissions** and is intended to sit alongside our EcoVadis assessment and the annual CDP submission. The policy is reviewed quarterly.

## 2. Reserve rules

We set aside a monthly reserve as a percentage of spend, broken down by category:

- Business travel: **3.5% of spend** (all flights, train, hotel, taxi).
- Team meals / restaurants: **2% of spend**.
- Procurement of hardware and office supplies: **1.5% of spend**.
- Cloud infrastructure and SaaS subscriptions: **1% of spend**.
- Professional services, consulting, and legal: **0.8% of spend**.

Anything that falls outside these categories uses a **per-tonne formula**: €80 for every tonne of CO₂e we estimate. (Internal guidance calls this "tonnes avoided pricing" — yes, we know that's not quite accurate terminology; treat as €/kg CO₂e.)

## 3. Approval and caps

- **Approval threshold:** any monthly allocation above **€400** must be approved by the finance director.
- **Monthly cap:** €6 000 maximum, hard cap. Anything beyond is escalated to the board.
- All transactions are logged; no category is ever hidden from the audit file.

## 4. Carbon credits

We buy **EU-registered credits only**. Our current mix aims for:

- Minimum **70% removal credits** (biochar, peatland, reforestation — with a preference for permanent technical removals).
- Up to 30% can be reduction-style credits if we can't find removal supply.

Project registries we trust: Puro.earth, Gold Standard, Peatland Code (Ireland).

## 5. Governance

This policy applies to Acme BV NL. International subsidiaries are out of scope until 2026. Our current CSRD status is **Wave 2** — first report due FY2026.

## 6. Known gaps

- We don't currently meter our office electricity directly. The landlord handles it, so utility bills don't come through our bunq account.
- No company vehicles as of writing.
- Remote-work energy use is not counted.
